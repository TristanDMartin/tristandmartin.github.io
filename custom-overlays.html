Custom Overlays

Learn how to create and implement professional stream overlays

Custom overlays are visual elements layered over your stream to provide information, branding, and aesthetic appeal. They can include webcam frames, alerts, chat boxes, and more. Creating personalized overlays helps establish your brand identity and engage your audience effectively.

🛠️ Creating Custom Overlays

  1. Designing Your Overlay
    • Graphic Design Tools: Use software like Adobe Photoshop or free alternatives like GIMP to design your overlays. These tools allow for precise customization and layering of elements.
    • Online Platforms: Platforms like Canva offer user-friendly interfaces with templates specifically for Twitch overlays. You can customize colors, text, and images to match your branding.
    • Templates: Utilize free or paid templates from websites like OWN3D.tv or Nerd or Die to get started quickly.
  2. Overlay Elements to Consider
    • Webcam Frames: Stylize your webcam feed with borders that match your theme.
    • Alerts: Design custom alerts for new followers, subscribers, or donations.
    • Chat Boxes: Integrate a chat display that complements your overlay design.
    • Stream Labels: Display recent events like top donations or latest followers.
    • Panels and Screens: Create starting soon, be right back, and ending screens to manage your stream transitions.

🎬 Implementing Overlays in OBS Studio

  • Add Image Sources: In OBS, add your overlay elements as image sources.
  • Positioning: Arrange the elements on your canvas to ensure they don't obstruct important content.
  • Layering: Use the layering system in OBS to stack elements appropriately, ensuring that alerts and webcam feeds appear above background elements.
  • Testing: Run test streams to ensure all elements function correctly and adjust as needed.
