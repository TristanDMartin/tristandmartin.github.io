Kick Stream Settings Guide

Optimize Your Stream for Quality, Stability & Viewer Experience

98% Success Rate
500+ Streamers Helped
120+ Guides Created

Whether you're on OBS, Streamlabs, or another tool, the right stream settings make all the difference in stream quality and discoverability.

🎯 Goal of This Guide

  • ✅ Deliver smooth 1080p60 gameplay
  • ✅ Avoid buffering & frame drops
  • ✅ Make your stream look professional
  • ✅ Ensure compatibility with Kick's encoder system

⚙️ Step 1: Recommended OBS Settings for Kick

Open OBS Studio → Go to Settings > Output

▶️ Output Tab

  • Output Mode: Advanced
  • Encoder: x264 (or NVIDIA NVENC for better GPU performance)
  • Rate Control: CBR
  • Bitrate:
    • 🟢 6,000 – 8,000 kbps (Recommended for 1080p60)
    • 🔒 Max supported by Kick is 8,500 kbps
  • Keyframe Interval: 2
  • CPU Usage Preset (x264 only): veryfast or faster
  • Profile: High

✅ Tip: If you're on NVENC (NVIDIA GPU), use "Quality" preset and "High" profile.

📐 Video Tab

Go to Settings > Video

  • Base (Canvas) Resolution: 1920x1080
  • Output (Scaled) Resolution: 1920x1080 (or 1280x720 for low-end PCs)
  • Downscale Filter: Lanczos (sharpest scaling)
  • FPS: 60 (or 30 if on low hardware)

🔊 Audio Tab

Go to Settings > Audio

  • Sample Rate: 48 kHz
  • Desktop Audio: Set to your system's default
  • Mic/Auxiliary Audio: Set your main microphone

In Advanced Audio Properties (in mixer):

  • Set Mic to "Monitor Off"
  • Ensure desktop/game audio is not clipping into red

🔗 Step 2: Get Your Kick Stream Key

Go to Kick.com → Click your profile picture → Creator Dashboard

Navigate to Settings > Stream Key

Copy:

  • Server (RTMP URL): rtmp://ingest.kick.com/live
  • Stream Key

🔒 DO NOT share this key — it gives full access to your stream.

🔄 Step 3: Add Kick Info to OBS

In OBS > Settings > Stream:

  • Service: Custom
  • Server: rtmp://ingest.kick.com/live
  • Stream Key: Paste what you copied from your Kick dashboard

Hit Apply, then OK.

🚀 Step 4: Stream Info & Going Live

Before you click "Start Streaming" in OBS:

  • Go to your Kick Dashboard
  • Edit Stream Title, Game Category, and Language
  • Upload a custom thumbnail for VOD visibility
  • Click Start Streaming in OBS → Go Live on Kick automatically

✅ Kick auto-detects your stream once OBS starts.

🧪 Performance Tips

Issue Fix Suggestion
Lag or dropped frames Lower bitrate to 6000 kbps
Audio out of sync Sync settings in Advanced Audio Properties
Pixelated video Set bitrate to 8000+ and resolution to 1080p
High CPU usage Use NVENC encoder (GPU-based)
