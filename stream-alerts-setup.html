Stream Alerts Setup

Learn how to create and customize engaging stream alerts

Stream alerts are a key component of interactive streaming. They notify you and your viewers when someone follows, subscribes, donates, raids, or performs other actions on your channel. Well-designed alerts enhance viewer engagement, acknowledge support, and reinforce your stream's brand identity.

If you've mastered basic alert setup, this guide helps you customize and elevate your alerts using powerful platforms like StreamElements, Streamlabs, and Sound Alerts.

Step-by-Step Guide to Set Up Stream Alerts

  1. Choose an Alert Service

    Popular alert platforms for streamers include:

    • StreamElements – Web-based overlay and alert editor with cloud-based tools.
    • Streamlabs – Full suite of alerts, widgets, themes, and donation management.
    • Sound Alerts – Twitch extension to play custom sound or video alerts, including channel point redemption.
    • Twitch Native Alerts – Built directly into Twitch's Creator Dashboard (great for simple alerts).
  2. Customize Your Alerts

    Customize the following elements:

    • Visuals: Upload custom animations (MP4/WebM), GIFs, or static images.
    • Audio: Choose sound effects (MP3/OGG) to match the action.
    • Text: Customize alert messages with variables like {user} or {amount}.
    • Timing and Animation: Adjust fade-in/out speeds, durations, and transitions.

    Most platforms offer drag-and-drop editors or JSON-based configurations for advanced control.

  3. Add Alerts to OBS Studio

    Once alerts are created:

    1. Copy Widget URL from your alert service.
    2. In OBS Studio, add a new Browser Source.
    3. Paste the URL, and set dimensions (commonly 800x600).
    4. Name and position the source appropriately on your layout.
    5. Use the service's Test Alerts feature to preview them live.

Where to Get Custom Alert Designs

Want your alerts to stand out? Try these resources:

  • Nerd or Die – Premium & Free Alert Packs
  • Elgato Marketplace – Stream Deck compatible alerts and overlays
  • OWN3D.tv – Themed alert bundles for Twitch, YouTube, and Facebook
  • StreamElements – Free Alert Maker with templates and effects

Final Tips

  • Use alert volume balancing – ensure sounds don't overpower your mic or game.
  • Design alerts to be on-brand – colors, fonts, and animations should reflect your style.
  • Use alerts strategically – avoid cluttering the screen with too many pop-ups.
  • Periodically refresh your design – keeps the experience fresh for returning viewers.
