Microphone Setup

Professional audio setup for streaming

Choose the Right Microphone

For beginners, a USB microphone is often the easiest to set up and use. They plug directly into your computer and don't require additional equipment like audio interfaces.

Popular Options
  • Blue Yeti
  • Audio-Technica ATR2100x
  • Samson Q2U

Position Your Microphone Correctly

Proper microphone placement is crucial for clear audio. Follow these key guidelines:

  • Distance: Keep the microphone about 6 to 12 inches away from your mouth.
  • Angle: Position it slightly off-axis to reduce plosive sounds (like "p" and "b" sounds).
  • Mounting: Use a boom arm or stand to keep the microphone steady and reduce handling noise.

Configure Your Audio Settings

Optimize your streaming software settings for the best audio quality:

  • Input Device: In your streaming software (like OBS or Streamlabs), select your microphone as the input device.
  • Gain Levels: Adjust the gain so your voice is clear without peaking. Aim for levels that stay in the green to low yellow range on your audio meter.
  • Monitoring: Use headphones to monitor your audio and prevent echo or feedback.

Enhance Your Audio Quality

Consider using these filters to improve your sound quality:

  • Noise Suppression: Reduces background noise.
  • Compressor: Balances the dynamic range of your audio.
  • Equalizer (EQ): Adjusts frequency levels to enhance voice clarity.
